# Candid Bike Conversation Rerun Direction

Use this direction before rerunning the Meal Prep Revolution bike conversation clips.

## Core Feel

This should feel like two busy Newport Beach moms quietly sharing a useful life cheat code while they keep riding recumbent bikes at the club. It should not feel like they are presenting to a third person, yelling a CTA, or performing an ad read.

## Character Continuity Lock

- Character A: brunette ponytail, same face, same dark green or neutral upscale Alo/Vuori-inspired activewear, no visible logos, no sunglasses, no eyewear.
- Character B: blonde beach-wave hair, same face, same taupe/white upscale Alo/Vuori-inspired activewear, no visible logos, no sunglasses, no eyewear.
- Keep the same gym, same bikes, same bike positions, same lighting, same wardrobe, same hairstyles, same age range, and same body types across every scene.

## Bike And Body Movement

- Both women remain seated on side-by-side recumbent bikes that face the same direction.
- Both bikes stay parallel and fixed in the same layout.
- Both women gently pedal throughout the conversation.
- Add natural subtle movement: slight shoulder rhythm from pedaling, small breath shifts, relaxed posture changes, tiny smiles, brief friend-to-friend glances.
- Keep movement realistic and restrained so lip sync remains clean.
- No dramatic gestures, no big hand waving, no exaggerated acting, no stiff frozen posture.

## Camera Direction

- Use candid over-the-shoulder shot-reverse-shot coverage.
- Camera B: Character B is sharp and speaking; Character A's brunette shoulder/pony tail is blurred on the right edge or lower-right foreground.
- Camera A: Character A is sharp and speaking; Character B's blonde shoulder/hair is blurred in the bottom-left foreground.
- The camera feels like it is quietly overhearing the conversation, not acting as a person being spoken to.
- The active speaker looks toward her friend, not into the lens.
- Keep the active speaker's face, eyes, and mouth fully visible.
- Camera stays locked enough for lip sync.

## CTA Treatment

- Do not show Character B yelling or performing the CTA on the bike.
- Use Character B's CTA audio as voiceover over the actual website screenshot end card.
- The website screenshot remains visually unchanged.
- This lets the bike conversation stay natural while the final frame becomes the clear conversion moment.

## Avoid

- Bikes facing each other.
- Direct-to-camera delivery.
- Third-person presenter energy.
- Two people visibly speaking in the same shot.
- Listener mouth movement while speaker talks.
- Outfit or hair changes.
- Sunglasses or eyewear.
- Stationary frozen bodies with no pedaling.
- Excessive movement that breaks lip sync.
